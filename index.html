02

How ATLAS Shield Works

ATLAS Shield uses a multi-layer scan architecture to detect spyware, stalkerware, and other malicious threats on your device. Here is how each layer operates:

Layer 1 - Whitelist and Anti-Tunneling

Filters known safe connections to reduce false positives. Detects attempts to tunnel malicious traffic through trusted services.

Layer 2 - IP IOC Matching

Matches active network connections against a local database of known malicious IP addresses sourced from Citizen Lab, CISA KEV, and AlienVault OTX.

Layer 3 - Domain IOC Matching

Checks DNS queries and domain connections against known malicious domains associated with spyware command-and-control infrastructure.

Layer 4 - Behavioral Analysis

Analyzes app behavior patterns and network activity to detect suspicious patterns that may indicate zero-day threats not yet in any IOC database.

App Hash and Permission Scanning

Scans installed application hashes and permission requests against known spyware signatures to identify malicious or compromised applications.

All layers operate entirely on your device. No scan results, threat data, or analysis outputs are transmitted to any external server.

03

VPN Service Usage

ATLAS Shield uses Android's VpnService API to create a local-only VPN tunnel on your device. This is essential for real-time network traffic inspection.

Important: This is NOT a Traditional VPN

Unlike commercial VPN services, ATLAS Shield does NOT route your traffic through any external server. The VPN runs entirely on your device as a local traffic filter. Your internet traffic goes directly to its intended destination - we simply inspect the metadata (IP addresses and domain names) to check against our threat intelligence database before allowing the connection to proceed.

Threat Intelligence Sources

ATLAS Shield maintains a local database of Indicators of Compromise (IOCs) sourced from the following reputable cybersecurity organizations:

SourceTypeDescription
Citizen LabResearchAcademic research lab tracking digital espionage and nation-state spyware

These IOC databases are bundled with the app and synced periodically via encrypted updates. Only IOC data (malicious IPs, domains, and app hashes) is downloaded - no user data is uploaded during this process.

Permissions We Request

ATLAS Shield requests only the permissions strictly necessary for its security functions:

  • 🔗
    BIND_VPN_SERVICE Required to create the local VPN tunnel for real-time network traffic inspection. No data is routed externally.
  • 📦
    QUERY_ALL_PACKAGES Required to scan installed applications against known spyware signatures and malicious app hashes.
  • 🌐
    INTERNET Required to sync IOC threat intelligence database updates. No personal data is transmitted.
  • 🔔
    POST_NOTIFICATIONS Required to alert you when threats are detected or when the VPN monitor status changes.
  • ⚙️
    FOREGROUND_SERVICE Required to keep the Guardian VPN Monitor running reliably in the background for continuous protection.
  • 🔋
    REQUEST_IGNORE_BATTERY_OPTIMIZATIONS Requested to prevent Android from stopping the security monitoring service during battery optimization.
